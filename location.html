Paris, also known as the "City of Love," is undoubtedly one of the most famous and fascinating cities in the world. The French capital entices with some of the most beautiful sights, including the iconic Eiffel Tower, the magnificent Notre-Dame Cathedral, the imposing Arc de Triomphe, and the famous Champs-Elysées.Art lovers should definitely visit the Musée d'Orsay or the Louvre to admire masterpieces from various epochs. In the immediate vicinity of Paris lies the imposing Palace of Versailles, which captivates millions of visitors from around the world with its magnificent architecture and splendid gardens. For those who want to immerse themselves in a fairy-tale world, a visit to Disneyland Paris is an absolute highlight. Paris is also known for its exquisite cuisine, elegant boulevards, and fashionable atmosphere, making the city a true paradise for gourmets and fashion enthusiasts.

Marseille, the second largest city in France and former European Capital of Culture, is located in the south of the country on the Mediterranean coast. The city captivates with a variety of museums, street art, art galleries, as well as lively bars and cafes.The heart of Marseille is its port, where fishermen land and sell their fresh catch daily. From the impressive Basilica of Notre-Dame de la Garde, you can enjoy a fascinating view over the city. Experience authentic French lifestyle in Marseille's old town with its narrow streets, traditional markets, and lively cafes. If you're looking for a break from city life, a visit to the Calanques National Park is recommended, where you can explore the breathtaking cliffs and coves on a boat tour or hike.

Strasbourg, the capital of Alsace, blends French and German culture and is renowned for its impressive Gothic cathedral, one of the tallest churches in the world.During your visit, be sure to try delicious culinary specialties such as Choucroute Garnie, a dish of sauerkraut and potatoes, as well as the traditional Flammkuchen. Let yourself be enchanted by fascinating sights such as the Palais des Rohan, the picturesque district of Petite France, and the historic center of Grande Île. The city captivates with its picturesque half-timbered houses, charming canals, and its UNESCO World Heritage-listed historic center. Furthermore, Strasbourg plays a significant political role in Europe as the seat of the European Parliament.
